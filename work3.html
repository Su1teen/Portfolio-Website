003 : Volunteering and MUN

As a student at Astana IT University and NIS School, I have actively engaged in volunteering opportunities within the IT community, which have not only enriched my technical skills but also fostered a spirit of giving back. One such avenue is my involvement in the CITech forum, where I dedicated my time and expertise to assist fellow students and tech enthusiasts in troubleshooting IT-related queries. Volunteering in IT forums has allowed me to stay updated with the latest industry trends while honing my problem-solving abilities by addressing diverse challenges faced by forum members.

Additionally, I eagerly participated in numerous IT hackathons, including Samgau and Intell Hackathons, where I collaborated with like-minded peers to develop innovative tech solutions within a constrained timeframe. These hackathons provided an ideal platform for applying classroom knowledge to real-world problems, pushing the boundaries of creativity and teamwork. Not only did I contribute to creating cutting-edge projects during these events, but I also fostered strong networks within the IT community, inspiring continuous learning and professional growth.

Beyond the realm of IT, I extended my volunteering efforts to organize Model United Nations (MUN) conferences in Aktobe. MUN conferences are vital platforms for students to develop critical skills such as diplomacy, negotiation, and public speaking. By assisting in organizing these conferences, I played a role in empowering young minds to engage in global issues and become future leaders in their communities. Through my multifaceted volunteering experiences in IT forums, hackathons, and MUN conferences, I have gained a profound appreciation for the transformative power of technology and education, solidifying my commitment to making a positive impact in both the IT industry and society at large.
